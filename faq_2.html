FREQENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

When and where is Visions?

Visions will take place on April 17, 2015 and is hosted by the University of North Carolina Wilmington Film Studies Department. Main screenings and conferences are held in the Lumina Theater on UNCW's campus. If you are an international or national undergraduate planning on submitting to Visions, contact submissions@visionsfilm.org.

Can I submit more than one film or paper?

You can submit as many papers or films as you like! There is no cap in place, so if you have multiple projects you feel strongly about, we encourage you to share them with us. Each film submission will need its own registration fee.

How do I submit?

There are two options for submission. This year Visions Film Festival & Conference will accept film via Film Freeway. A link to Film Freeway as well as instructions on how to use Film Freeway can be found under the “submit” tab. Presentation proposals will be accepted via email to submissions@visionsfilm.org. When submissions open, forms for papers can be found as .pdf attachments under the “submit” tab. If your film is selected, we will request a copy of your film in an H.264 file. Instructions on exporting can be found under the “submit” tab.

What is the time limit for a film?

Visions programs films that are 25 minutes or shorter in duration. Submitted films that exceed this limit will not be accepted.

How much are registration fees?

Visions badges get you into a full day of events, including: two film blocks, two conference blocks, the keynote address, the video race, morning and evening receptions, the video installation exhibit AND the after party. All for just $10 ($150 value!). Individual tickets can be purchased on the day of the event for $5 at Sharky’s Box Office.

What Should I Bring For The 1 Hour/1 Take Video Race?

Visions supplies each team with a camera. Some folks bring additional equipment, costumes, props, etc. It's up to you! More information please contact our Video Race Coordinators, Dustin Fleischmann and Ashley Morgan at hospitality@visionsfilm.org.

I am coming from out of town. Where can I stay?

We will do everything within our power to assist our fellow filmmakers in making it to Wilmington. We can set you up with a hosting student, or you can take advantage of discounted rates from our hotel sponsors. If you have questions about traveling and accommodations, contact our Hospitality Coordinator at hospitality@visionsfilm.org.

Is Visions Handicap Accessible?

Yes. For disability accommodation operations needs, please contact our Facilities and Guest Information Coordinator, Robert O'Connor at operations@visionsfilm.org.

How Old Do I Have To Be To Get Into The After Party?

The after party is 18 and over. More information about this year's after party and the venue will be posted on the after party page as it becomes available.

Is The Festival Age Appropriate?

The first screening block of the day is family friendly. Later screenings are reserved for material that may not be suitable for all audiences.

2013 Film and Paper Submission Guidelines

The mission of the Visions Film Festival & Conference (the "Festival") is to acknowledge the work of future imagemakers, storytellers, and scholars, as well as to create platforms for undergraduate filmmakers and film scholars to reach an audience and network within an international film community. Submitting a film or paper abstract constitutes acceptance of the following:

KEY DATES
October 6, 2012 – Submissions Open
December 7, 2012 – Early Deadline for Films
January 7, 2013 – Normal Deadline for Films; Abstracts Deadline for Papers
February 8, 2013 – Official Deadline for Papers (selected abstracts)
February 15, 2013 – Late Deadline for Films
April 5, 2013 – 3rd Annual Visions Film Festival & Conference in Wilmington, NC ("Visions 3")

ENTRY FEES
Early Deadline (12/7/12) – Films US$15
Normal Deadline (1/7/13) – Films US$25; Abstracts Free
Late Deadline (2/15/13) – Films only US$35

Note: Entry fees are in U.S. Dollars, are per film, and are non-refundable. The Festival reserves the right to disqualify a submission, without refund of any kind, if eligibility requirements are not met including if eligibility status changes post-submission.


Film Entries

ELIGIBILTY
To be eligible for consideration:
  1. Entrants must fully comply with these Submission Guidelines, including all deadlines, film length, entry material, and other requirements.
  2. Films may be submitted in one of any of the following categories: narrative, documentary, experimental, or animation. If a hybrid, choose one category on all forms.
  3. Submitted films must be no longer than 25 minutes in duration.
  4. All significant production roles (director, producer, screenwriter, cinematographer, editor, etc.) must have been completed by undergraduate students.
  5. Non-English language works must have English subtitles at the time submitted. Dialogue lists will not be accepted.
HOW TO ENTER
Fill out the official Visions "Film Entry Form" (.pdf) and mail with all required entry materials (see below).

ENTRY MATERIALS
  1. A printed "Film Entry Form" (.pdf) properly completed.
  2. The entry fee(s), payable by cash, check, or money order.
  3. A photocopy of your student ID.
  4. Two (2) DVD/CD-R copies of the work as a Quicktime file (H.264), formatted for multi-zone or Zone 1 (North America) and labeled with the work's title, running time, director(s) name(s). Check that each submitted DVD/CD-R plays in its entirety before mailing to the address below. Also, do not put adhesive labels on the discs unless it is a professional output job (if burning yourself, use a permanent marker to write necessary information).
Note: It is the Entrant's responsibility to ensure all entry materials arrive by the selected deadline. Entrants may email submissions@visionsfilm.org to find out if the Festival received the submission.

MULTIPLE ENTRIES
Each entered film must be submitted on its own DVD/CD-R and accompanied by its own printed "Film Entry Form" and entry fee; please do not send a compilation DVD/CD-R composed of works for separate entries.

ACCEPTED FILMS
Festival programmers select and invite all films to be presented at the Festival. Invited filmmakers will be notified by email or phone via the information provided on the submitted entry form. Make sure to update us with new contact information.

If your work is selected, you must fulfill the following requirements by the date(s) designated by the Festival:
  1. You must execute a release agreement wherein you (i) accept responsibility for obtaining any and all clearances necessary to exhibit your film at Visions 3; (ii) warrant that you have the rights necessary to exhibit your film at Visions 3; and (iii) idemnify and hold harmless the Visions Film Festival & Conference, the University of North Carolina Wilmington, its affiliates, and subsidaries and any of their respective directors, officers, employees, and representatives against any claim arising out of the exhibition of your film at Visions 3.
  2. You must provide all press and publication assets specified in the "Press Materials Checklist".

Paper Entries

ELIGIBILITY
To be eligible for consideration:
  1. Entrants must fully comply with these Submission Guidelines, including all deadlines, entry material, and other requirements.
  2. Paper topics may focus on any aspect of film and media history, criticism, or theory.
  3. Entrants must submit a 300 to 400 word abstract by January 7, 2013, for preliminary screening. If the abstract is selected to procee, the final paper must be submitted by February 8, 2013, to be considered.
HOW TO ENTER
Email your entry form and abstract to submissions@visionsfilm.org.

Apply online at www.visionsfilm.org/submit and mail all required entry materials (see below).

ENTRY MATERIALS
  1. A "Paper Submission Form" (.pdf) properly completed.
  2. A 300 to 400 word abstract attached as a .pdf or .doc file.
  3. A short author bio also attached as a .pdf or .doc file.

ACCEPTED PAPERS
All submissions are reviewed and programmed by a committee of undergraduate film students under the supervision of a faculty advisor. Festival programmers select and invite all papers to be presented at the Festival. Invited students will be notified by email or phone via the information provided on the submitted entry form. Make sure to update us with new contact information.

Invited students will be given 15 minute slots to present their paper and during that time may include technical aids such as DVD clips or PowerPoint to accompany their presentation. The Festival will arrange and reimburse presenters for their full cost of travel and lodging to Wilmington.


Awards

Previously accepted filmmakers and scholars have been eligible to win awards when their work was selected as exemplary in any of our categories. Visions honors Excellence in Narrative, Experiemental, Documentary, and Animation, as well as Excellence in Scholarship. In addition, there is an Audience Choice Award for Filmmaking, and an Audience Choice Award for Scholarship. The highest honor, the Visionary Award, recognizes one student work that stands out as a paradigm of the Visions mission statement: it is fresh, imaginative, boundary-pushing, and exhibits a passion for film.

Awards page.

Previous award winners have received gifts from DigiEffects, the Cucalorus Film Festival, Cinelab, Camera Motion Research, Corel, Pro-8, Working Films, Audio-Technica, Wavom, American Cinematographer, and Film Matters magazine.

Of course, in addition to tangible awards, Visions offers all visiting filmmakers and scholars a wonderful opportunity to network with other students and members of the film community who share a fervent interest in and love for film.

    In 2012, cash awards totaled $195,000 in addition to over $250,000 of in-kind services and products and art work.
    Unless otherwise agreed by the Festival in writing, awards will be given to the winning film director (as identified in the Film Information Form) on behalf of the film (except for Best Screenplay, Best Cinematography, Best Actor, Best Actress, and Best Editing) and will be fulfilled approximately 10-12 weeks after the closing date of the TFF13.
    Award eligibility is subject to any restrictions or conditions imposed by applicable laws and regulations (international, federal, state or otherwise), and the award recipient is responsible for all applicable taxes, customs, tariffs, insurance and similar charges or costs (if any).
    All new Features (except those only shown in the “Gala” section or as a “Special Screening” or “Special Event”) are eligible for the “Audience Award,” regardless of section.
    2013 Awards listed in the above Festival sections and Awards sections are subject to change.

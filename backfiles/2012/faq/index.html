 FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Where are the submission forms?
Both paper and film submission forms can be found as .pdf attachments on the submissions page, to the right of the screen. There are also instructions on exporting your film as an H.264 file, which is the preferred format of the festival. 

Can you submit more than one film or paper?
You can submit as many papers or films as you like! There is no cap in place, so if you have multiple projects you feel strongly about we encourage you to share them with us. Remember, though, that each film submission will need its own registration fee.

What is the time limit for a film?
VISIONS has a tentative time limit of 20 minutes for any one film. You can submit films with a longer run time, and exceptions can be made for outstanding works of art. However, anything over 30 minutes will not be screened. As a good rule of thumb, the shorter your film is the better.

What is the page limit for a paper?
VISIONS has no specific page limit for conference papers. However, we do have a presentation time limit of 15 minutes. Depending on your delivery and the media you plan to use, your page limit may vary. We have found, though, that a 5 to 6 page limit is usually suitable.

When and where is VISIONS?
VISIONS will be hosted at the University of North Carolina Wilmington on March 30, 2012, in Wilmington, North Carolina. Main screenings and conferences are held in the Lumina Theater on UNCW's campus. If you are an international or national student planning on submitting to VISIONS, find out about travel accommodations here.

How much are registration fees?
Entrance fees for films and papers are listed on our submissions page under "Important Deadlines." For films, entry fees are $10 for early submissions, $15 for regular submissions, and $25 for late submissions. There are no entrance fees for papers. There are also no registration fees for the One Hour One Take video race.

I'm coming from out of town. Where can I stay?
We'll do everything we can to help our fellow undergraduates make the trip to Wilmington. We can set you up with a hosting student, or you can take advantage of discounted rates from our hotel sponsors. If you have questions about traveling and accommodations, contact our Hospitality Coordintor at hospitality@visionsfilm.org.

What should I bring for the One Hour One Take video race?
Bring whatever equipment you think you could make a video with, a winning attitude, and a desire to mix, mingle and create with a community of undergraduate filmmakers. Don't have a fancy camera? No worries! You can use the camera on your cellphone if you are so inclined. Remember, the point of the video race is to connect with other filmmakers in a relaxed and creative environment between our screenings and conferences. Be flexible and have fun with it!

Is VISIONS age appropriate?
The first screening block of the day is family friendly. Later screenings are reserved for material that may not be appropriate for younger viewers.

Is VISIONS handicap accessible?
Yes.

How much are passes to attend the event?
VISIONS is happy to announce that we will be hosting the event absolutely free of charge. Our mission is to bring together the future filmmakers and scholars of our generation with an audience of film enthusiasts. All those interested in an event that showcases imaginative and insightful works of art are welcomed to join us.

How old do I have to be to get into the after party?
The after party is 18 and over. More information about this year's after party and the venue will be posted on the after party page as it becomes available.

2013 Submission Guidelines

The mission of the Visions Film Festival & Conference (the "Festival") is to acknowledge the work of future imagemakers, storytellers, and scholars, as well as to create platforms for undergraduate filmmakers and film scholars to reach an audience and network within an international film community. Submitting a film or paper abstract constitutes acceptance of the following:

KEY DATES

October 6, 2012 – Submissions Open
December 7, 2012 – Early Deadline for Films
January 7, 2013 – Normal Deadline for Films; Abstracts Deadline for Papers
February 8, 2013 – Official Deadline for Papers (selected abstracts)
February 15, 2013 – Late Deadline for Films
April 5, 2013 – 3rd Annual Visions Film Festival & Conference in Wilmington, NC ("Visions 3")

ENTRY FEES

Early Deadline (12/7/12) – Films US$15
Normal Deadline (1/7/13) – Films US$25; Abstracts Free (US$0)
Late Deadline (2/15/13) – Films only US$35

Note: Entry fees are in U.S. Dollars, are per film, and are non-refundable. The Festival reserves the right to disqualify a submission, without refund of any kind, if eligibility requirements are not met including if eligibility status changes post-submission.

Film Entries

ELIGIBILITY

To be eligible for consideration:
  1. Entrants must fully comply with these Submission Guidelines, including all deadlines, film length, entry material, and other requirements.
  2. Films may be submitted in one of any of the following categories: narrative, documentary, experimental, or animation. If a hybrid, choose one category on all forms.
  3. Submitted films must be no longer than 25 minutes in duration.
  4. All significant production roles (director, producer, screenwriter, cinematographer, editor, etc.) must have been completed by undergraduate students.
  5. Non-English language works must have English subtitles at the time submitted. Dialogue lists will not be accepted.

HOW TO ENTER

Fill out the official Visions "Film Entry Form" (.pdf) and mail with all required entry materials (see below).

ENTRY MATERIALS

  1. A printed "Film Entry Form" (.pdf) properly completed.
  2. The entry fee(s), payable by cash, check, or money order.
  3. A photocopy of your student ID.
  4. Two (2) DVD copies of the work as a Quicktime file (H.264) labeled with the work's title, running time, director(s) name(s). Check that each submitted DVD plays in its entirety before mailing to the address below. Also, do not put adhesive labels on the discs unless it is a professional output job (if burning yourself, use a permanent marker to write necessary information).
  5. If submitting with a Vimeo account, indicate the URL and password on your Entry Form and mail in with your entry fee by the chosen deadline. All online videos must be password protected.
Note: It is the Entrant's responsibility to ensure all entry materials arrive by the selected deadline. Entrants may email submissions@visionsfilm.org to find out if the Festival received the submission.

MULTIPLE ENTRIES

Each entered film must be submitted on its own DVD and accompanied by its own printed "Film Entry Form" and entry fee; please do not send a compilation DVD composed of works for separate entries.

ACCEPTED FILMS

Festival programmers select and invite all films to be presented at the Festival. Invited filmmakers will be notified by email or phone via the information provided on the submitted entry form. Make sure to update us with new contact information.

If your work is selected, you must fulfill the following requirements by the date(s) designated by the Festival:
  1. You must execute a release agreement wherein you (i) accept responsibility for obtaining any and all clearances necessary to exhibit your film at Visions 3; (ii) warrant that you have the rights necessary to exhibit your film at Visions 3; and (iii) idemnify and hold harmless the Visions Film Festival & Conference, the University of North Carolina Wilmington, its affiliates, and subsidaries and any of their respective directors, officers, employees, and representatives against any claim arising out of the exhibition of your film at Visions.
  2. You must provide all press and publication assets specified in the "Press Materials Checklist".

PRESS MATERIALS CHECKLIST

  1. Logo for the film, if available.
  2. Filmmaker contact.
  3. Film synopsis.
  4. Any interesting details about the production (behind-the-scenes stills/footage, interviews, anecdotes about the production, etc.).
  5. Filmmaker bio and photo of director and all key crew members.
  6. Credits.
  7. Other press clips, if available.
  8. Film stills, one-sheet, and any official trailers.

SHIPPING ADDRESS

All submissions must be sent to:
Visions Film Festival & Conference
University of North Carolina Wilmington
C/O Assistant Professor Shannon Silva
601 South College Road
Wilmington, NC 28407

Paper Entries

ELIGIBILTIY

To be eligible for consideration:
  1. Entrants must fully comply with these Submission Guidelines, including all deadlines, entry materials, and other requirements.
  2. Paper topics may focus on any aspect of film and media history, criticism, or theory.
  3. Entrants must submit a 300 to 400 word abstract by January 7, 2013, for preliminary screening. If selected to proceed, the final paper must be submitted by February 8, 2013, to be considered.

HOW TO ENTER

Fill out the official Visions "Abstract Entry Form" and email it with all entry materials (see below) to submissions@visionsfilm.org.

ENTRY MATERIALS

  1. An "Abstract Submission Form" (.pdf) properly completed.
  2. A 300 to 400 word abstract and brief author biography, attached as a .pdf or .doc file.

MULTIPLE ENTRIES

Multiple entries are accepted and encouraged.

SELECTED ABSTRACTS

If your abstract is selected to proceed, you will be notified by the email provided on your Entry Form. The final paper must then be sent in by the requested date in order to be considered. A request for your paper does not guarantee your acceptance into the Festival.

ACCEPTED PAPERS

All submissions are reviewed and programmed by a committee of undergraduate film students under the supervision of a faculty advisor. Festival programmers select and invite all papers to be presented at the Festival. Invited students will be notified by email or phone via the information provided on the submitted Entry Form. Make sure to update us with new contact information.

Invited students will be given 15 minute slots to present their paper and during that time may include technical aids such as DVD clips or PowerPoint to accompany their presentation. The Festival will arrange and reimburse presenters for the cost of travel and lodging.

Awards

Previously accepted filmmakers and scholars have been eligible to win awards when their work was selected as exemplary in any of our categories. Visions honors excellence in narrative, documentary, experimental, and animation, as well as excellence in scholarship. In addition, attending filmmakers and film scholars are eligible for the Audience Choice Awards for filmmaking and scholarship. The Visionary Award, our highest honor, recognizes one student work that stands out as a paradigm of the Visions mission statement.

Previous award winners have received gifts from DigiEffects, the Cucalorus Film Festival, Cinelab, Camera Motion Research, Corel, Pro-8, Working Films, Audio-Technica, Wacom, American Cinematographer, and Film Matters magazine.
